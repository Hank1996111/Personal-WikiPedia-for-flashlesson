**The Rundown:** OpenAI just **[rolled out](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.eCbm_1zon7G0lMoXTECWa-IUY9yqSc2cx0km5OJXo-MUbTI-iYkgCHQhc1-02DhnMh_B0S_9b0Yk19EYzyjdlOyyN5a4trn4qE-uW8l85SzP-YNOr68UGiEsX8RcKYUw8WI9llHZ-a7CIgRDk6HW00tUIwsgOLYN5AxjH8EOXvlHfWgvt9pvyRvHyRIRCQtvGfh63gncFH4KKFfBn78_fxIfAR5LXsdKnMg0ai2EV2YB2KM-e954kk7hQ4qY5VV1pJhrR6gzlgS3WDzmAGTr9Q/4im/Y3Kt4oD3QLaOuW-7LmWYOA/h20/h001.dzKyb-z7PuQHEGD5_oT9A71QClCRbYKe5UfDF4eyN8g)** Study Mode for ChatGPT, a new feature designed to guide students through problems step-by-step, using Socratic questions and feedback instead of just providing solutions.


**The details:**


- Study Mode uses custom instructions created with teaching experts to ask guiding questions and provide interactive learning instead of direct answers.

- The AI guides learners with interactive prompts, hints, and knowledge checks to encourage active participation and deeper understanding.

- ChatGPT will actively resist requests for quick solutions in this mode, instead redirecting students back to the learning process.

- The rollout begins immediately for Free, Plus, Pro, and Team users, with educational institutions receiving access within the next few weeks.
