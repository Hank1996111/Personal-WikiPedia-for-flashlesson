**The Rundown:** Netflix executives **[declared](https://elinke1c.daily.therundown.ai/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMfyzbPJdh2qAj8kGiBAmrmvZ4tQR6IZQBfmkLIQx25uvWamwqnBuzpE-XwA6VKvSNtFSuS5JiD4NSrzr9agrgXhuRD8CUrn9hHPAT1EGZJgjz7EVqiutrIlBB2bTAdCJbtcSVh1KPXf15uc3qPpvlnRoZy0iYXXYNJJHuoCFPNGAukkmH6jlpTvHLRk85CsL-k2lACOinISismGZ25jR5uM9RfRgQiFlAUchdYRhRyA9FOBB14NO1OJ3LkKtg3PRL_3EtwvbuPzEY8rEotaFdBeFzuD47cV8TRNAsBfpDchJwYwRaZiNexsIhi9amM3weOg/4l0/eD8nFtQnQpiFBKTfE5-Cjw/h23/h001.fgmbDdGxkla_41R4YBedifTQv3PGTA4oZjSmyXEhF_0)** that the streaming giant is going “all in” on AI during the company’s earnings call this week across both business operations and content production, coming despite the broader industry’s continued skepticism.


**The details:**


- Netflix outlined plans to deploy AI across recommendations, advertising, and production workflows, **[saying](https://elinke1c.daily.therundown.ai/ss/c/u001.7FjCl1Hhb45GEizGv1NNbH2Fvf6pkMGd3vWDQegVWbBZF5vgLkg3o_CiFhlFKMznUCeeFTI9tz0zv2ZiB2KdcYd-4TxOrE2fW9p6UOXqUNFrgcuu1a2fYoZDYRFCHEb3pz27QGJNiaDVFJSw3d7QFTgm4XWLqVD3GNR7qNuNob6vC2xP3nBDrOmknvhFgDpEe1mJd5jh9Pl2S_SjVyQFG9JcCK97N1DYzEpA-Fd6_7Ad9Z7nUiARSc2-r53v2nGG8eRMXxEGgvxzd2Q9TnvYWHLiOTeRhc5vD-KbZMrSdQwznT7_3Q1BqeXBUBRdsMxevdMzJnbRFaiVXZTO-NkG8Q/4l0/eD8nFtQnQpiFBKTfE5-Cjw/h24/h001.RITCLw-PkBN6LSfNt1FvzWzQ2O-oiHvFTca6MNz_Tp8)** the company is “well positioned” for the AI boom.

- Several Netflix productions have already incorporated the tech for uses like age-reversing and experimenting with wardrobe and set concept ideation.

- CEO Ted Sarandos said he isn’t worried about AI replacing creativity, believing the tech will help creators “tell stories better, faster, and in new ways.”

- He also said, “AI can give creatives better tools to enhance experiences for our members, but it doesn’t automatically make you a great storyteller if you’re not.”


**Why it matters:** Between AI actors, feuds with OpenAI over Sora, constant battles with Hollywood unions, and backlash from fans, AI’s transition into entertainment has been anything but smooth. But as advances continue, the balancing act between companies like Netflix, talent, and fans will only get more difficult.
