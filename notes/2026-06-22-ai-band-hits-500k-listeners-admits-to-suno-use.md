**The Rundown:** A mysterious band that went viral on Spotify with over 500,000 monthly listeners has been **[revealed](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMf7O_UFE0kdclB8XF5XfbCsG0CITNoZ8pkNXLCXieDXMlZtSL3O_3QQG-Be1ElExYPuV_YctI_7tKRI28UHVkOn4rHb6SPOJgGgtsV862rTNu_xhoK6rapbjHjrlPST0ZMpiQ6oL-mlWMcEQGF1P4nvWn4jUi8nQxKCdCMnuQj3ZLyQep_nFmlo16csyFki2FyT_3A8AQsq2_HIqvrF-nDpor-PThHNd9sawT_nvOcOIVWnfPJ4XeNIOVTKIuaJfNKJSlkLis98Aup3-VE8yQ_hYwg-uUJA2xWb8s5YcQSwtyLWjSialNniXJxV1lxacrMg/4hv/5xQAiHSAQsa5b57meqKS9A/h6/h001.qXEBAijG5e-TTr6jGiqdkYCoys5AuwqQ3nHheuZ0aMY)** as an "art hoax" in an article from Rolling Stone, with music created using the AI generator Suno.


**The details:**


- The group's two albums appeared on streaming platforms in June with zero digital footprint, raising skepticism from Reddit users and musicians.

- Music platform Deezer flagged potential AI usage, but Spotify made no disclosure requirements, allowing the tracks to spread across 30+ playlists.

- The “band” initially **[said](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.6k0_SAz8nrOuu\_-LoNX1HbwcDIi17fVfvue8CDV2eHkVi9nPKSDdZjjnsGtg_MkCo1ZKHS32H085AHVhpMqsWKWVAxDXJWyA-\_tEy_NSSrcm4Y_FAMR-m19bcsEKFjrB3cSb32P_sAYeern8gF51hcoRGTMP34somO87KS2WoyaNrpW0SE7qgclb3C3O0aBLCNG1jEkt_h8WmRNEMtQdnVEC74-uCKjeXc-eQdoScFiofolLlNWUr3pvQWXmIUWAFhekZhSOzQ2RSgCqrxm_uvzKkHfSnCkhogD_LyY_LTc/4hv/5xQAiHSAQsa5b57meqKS9A/h7/h001.2yh9f1KgrrPMUQ3bVqYmsJ-ZGROP2vGSklYUBcJBFhY)** the AI claims are lazy and baseless on social media, with “adjunct member” Andrew Frelon calling it “marketing and trolling.”

- Frelon said Suno was used to create at least some of the tracks, leveraging its "Persona" feature to maintain a consistent vocal style.


**Why it matters:** While the music tracks and band identity clearly didn’t pass the human test this time, future models and outputs certainly will (and are likely already hiding in plain sight).
