**The Rundown:** Columbia University doctors **[achieved](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMf9dPeOgbqaHm-Y4uhItLwOqWeErMOAFRh_Y8VRtieRK2y0Reg-FlPpWNu0lHNXJgMdHhSMHY7Y4XUKGiQpRGNL0S-AY_GwxyfzoBzIw0OaprIV2wuptu5HodL4VvDxqZG9ANUC_hSCDavJbCx4xSm56bu5auL2-4CyDUqH9_WEj-1AE1D3LkfcygqiHKJmpP9aq6ShIkWie8nk7W-YA0Cxysi7i-FSwmIfE4jQ-mJg2F0rf3rX-PxQZLXF5TJw-EgqFfoI4m9wO-x1FSTR613f9in_9hA9y7gwWbBupMsrKB/4hw/M6UeTxZ4QuaTpQ8GVJtvVQ/h7/h001.s6CAqYkSV_OILPnyMbOuYksqgoELO5JsEhL6mMJo4ww)** the first pregnancy using an AI system called STAR, which helped a couple conceive after an 18-year struggle by discovering viable sperm in a man with severe infertility.


**The details:**


- STAR uses AI to scan semen samples from men with azoospermia, a condition with nearly zero measurable sperm, instead of the typical 200-300M cells.

- The system scanned 8M microscopic images in under an hour, locating 44 cells, whereas human technicians found zero after two days of searching.

- Columbia's team developed the approach over five years, adapting astrophysics algorithms for new stars to detect microscopic reproductive cells.

- STAR is only **[used](https://link.mail.beehiiv.com/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMf4tYQxcRLq34sKm2wHg0Ct6PdrqmYEzquSOoam154olNoNDWgR9cqWNhqEPqeqHF5E0KwlRqrLpAf-U45dm4lrjffO0fCzs7DjuVRQAGAbVRndrU-5mq7dNu3PiarpKVU1L04G8FfZvQbmx4WlhzzfEKE_tLkbCiCNTWsPiJS1u-abiK9fGiiF3_jjRBSM_eYXxerLH6gEG4o46fNn8ob4r4doPahXkxF3uWbVT_TTDPBneWS402wRtGxstvvSP_5Nqj4RWvy242uotOe0_7kxoq6gsKFYCH9dcn5bKQ11LFIDKNbMR5lCFXVek9xZysjRf-ICP80MPEc408DdN-qKrOVZInEJ0brfx5zO18JpJImHqJ468hwAnBjOkr3Rrpo59Qb5Uu8gX1EdDsp0kBf3k/4hw/M6UeTxZ4QuaTpQ8GVJtvVQ/h8/h001.wJ6pxt8f4ezopXQ-SgLOlc_6uQ1OHLH50JcrlS2Yt-U)** at the Columbia University Fertility Center for now, with an estimated $3K cost compared to as high as $15-30K for a single IVF cycle.


**Why it matters:** Fertility rates are plunging across the globe — and for many, the costs for expensive cycles of IVF treatments (which don’t guarantee success) are an insurmountable barrier. With STAR and new AI-driven systems, doctors can hopefully provide solutions to infertility at a more accessible price to hopeful parents.
