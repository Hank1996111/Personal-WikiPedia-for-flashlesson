**The Rundown:** Anthropic **[thwarted](https://elinke1c.daily.therundown.ai/ss/c/u001.Q334NVcZU4O6L6VKRz8ijF3rPclFOInaeXyJEFkO6PIVggh7BQTKxU719DDQ4tiU0Sc_zli023vjzyh0zyvxavPHBYVjUyJpKtoRA64Crg122RwFNyeJzKkYFa2oc47_Nx_p3UzgBV1BxMM65oZ6BEOWd24ff4lDeQdfv9FSCGKcJkbvxRPgG7H8SkEOowhZiM4ZWiazb8B2GSUu1e7AGAaYYbXLVpb71lQk37uyzziV4Hnc_zzDhjX7JRGOs2cdjkUMetWJKXgC3qx2zlYR3sAvosvTK0LbyY2LAxfUeDZK07SehMKAHYL6qTaEFLPzyFdkiwSwmVqeiASLRJ-dFv18BHV9PjscK5CPO7jWSxR9yFwMefSoaVooR1ez_sMX/4ll/ethw4QD9TbShRnZeXRyEhQ/h7/h001.Sa0inI5tP4PJJgV7e5tMKWS9MeDGdbiROVaUda-ws8Y)** what it believes is the first AI-driven cyber espionage campaign, after attackers were able to manipulate Claude Code to infiltrate dozens of organizations, with the model executing 80-90% of the attack autonomously.


**The details:**


- The September 2025 operation targeted roughly 30 tech firms, financial institutions, chemical manufacturers, and government agencies.

- The threat was assessed with ‘high confidence’ to be a Chinese state-sponsored group, using AI’s agentic abilities to an “unprecedented degree.”

- Attackers tricked Claude by splitting malicious tasks into smaller, innocent-looking requests, claiming to be security researchers pushing authorized tests.

- The attacks mark a major step up from Anthropic’s “vibe hacking” **[findings](https://elinke1c.daily.therundown.ai/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMf937MUSrYzK6JzB2n81ON3wprj7HIZSPjP_eG5XrRGU1K73z18ITXs7w72gv2OgykrL_C1vo4mivJjMwD7viYZYTb7FCfS7jPrnD5EJwtoHVSYQF0Q4gQCuuAN42BMsl_ThZ0R7CiQ8joN7BeDZ3rYm8JR8FbLCbPPBqqs_IvEt6hHKUinjporYjQ6L-xfePYv25ng0txwVj1CaCbF8gzqWbsghTk3Wuu81JjpgMyPc6WDOEZcowS4ENf5jalKiGCl7dFHAvSYaTQB73zUfmmDc/4ll/ethw4QD9TbShRnZeXRyEhQ/h8/h001.rgmQK1454CeXGQHEDx13c21LwGznSRz2j1W9NS6qtvU)** in June, now requiring minimal human oversight beyond strategic approval.


**Why it matters:** Anthropic calls this the “first documented case of a large-scale cyberattack executed without substantial human intervention”, and AI’s agentic abilities are creating threats that move and scale faster than ever. While AI capabilities can also help prevent them, security for organizations worldwide likely needs a major overhaul.
