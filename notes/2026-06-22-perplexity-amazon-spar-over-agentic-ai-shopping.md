**The details:**


- Perplexity **[revealed](https://elinke1c.daily.therundown.ai/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMf_opy6mIvpZNWVg6eiOhxpIfr1zQ8McK-YMUCsbF9VvvIc-yfeKAIKbNiij_mm__4WXKtU5MCBHKhPBkVSfzAacr0YL42qWqQXKrwDumqtictyZjMpTUKF_6vZI_uXQGndIA_fL-jgcMQ3sFSw2oGjPxa98Wu-giFIt6ie2XXkRxtgHv1kgs6NqLz8Z_hPdzSIQa5Kh0tkw6Cr0UmHL61acvCpfmBJWlhZIts3OdeasHF5kC0PMXjw_zJQsATAx-wg/4lc/DGkEmt6lTr26GU_Jduu_rA/h13/h001.aKKWvW55mwAyEqSFxOW6YNA72jW8F5TPQyO5VFgvOxc)** that Amazon sent an “aggressive legal threat” demanding that it prevent Comet users from using agents to shop on its platform.

- Amazon posted a **[blog](https://elinke1c.daily.therundown.ai/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMfyU1OZJ15_2x0B7lpFcaDYu4kMpylKmEkJCpCsGCPGZnBO8wlhNBAzNYRhJO-qB5E66a7afgGPgoaMslOyK4djOK2n4j0iWHYE0xTj3CxGBrSVhtxzPO9m0PhAnVjxbDYHIcgMbIUq1Efb3HazFds1mQvsUmY5_BpXSLejeWOwpS0VEjEJnQ0y14si8vjnUhFls1N1GXkO5dmZvChgVo04OgL8qWl8INyAjfrfvcoUXr_N-sqwd7S4UIfltpOXmB5Ob2qWxMFdu0e54LyjxozLRqSwr05qPVbRqROg5UoDYP/4lc/DGkEmt6lTr26GU_Jduu_rA/h14/h001.bxfK0PoXUtQHSN9VqKeqUZjztBiAlAaSO-m5pTOdWRs)** of its own, citing concerns over Perplexity’s “significantly degraded shopping and customer service experience”.

- Amazon has also blocked AI crawlers from OpenAI, Google, and Meta in recent months, while developing its own shopping tools like Rufus and "Buy For Me."

- Perplexity said it “will not be intimidated”, calling Amazon a “corporate bully” that is motivated by selling ads over user rights.


**Why it matters:** This is the start of a collision course between AI agents and platforms wanting to control the experience (or creating their own in-house agents that compete directly). With agents dependent on the open web to complete tasks, platforms closing access could throw a major wrench in the already clunky agentic process.
