**The Rundown:** AI ‘Godmother’ Fei-Fei Li’s World Labs just **[released](https://elinke1c.daily.therundown.ai/ss/c/u001.dSnm3kaGd0BkNqLYPjeMf3uHd_wg5Hhw7vcMjjnWTIG238XHWa20YfKX5R-2Rgbhtm3TiDiwYwPZPweT15cQfMXwnknb-d_Ktc_vAyrplHMgw1zflE0Cu7Fgz78v5AyCuCk25dZ5nuZOgindSZ47XntZDHK60LdDMpw3AS0-N7hGFGgREfYhbUBuSHZTf48U2yuzGvPpGOOkBwknxlKOkbtmWEr1BVT0skNTfuVABj7WgmNW15uB_I3c0g2ZBUwN3g1HtpMwTzVY9OyCGJ7pXA/4lk/5ry3T3uiS0axzLTYrAdPpA/h6/h001.H5adyVOuJF6A1AB-1zrWElPUULlJXfjd2_jnd7NoCa4)** Marble, its first commercial world model that generates persistent 3D environments from text, images, videos, or 3D layouts, positioning it ahead of rivals like Google's Genie and Decart.


**The details:**


- Users can both create new worlds via text, image, and video prompts or edit, combine, and expand on existing ones to make granular changes.

- The platform lets creators export worlds as Gaussian splats, meshes, or videos, allowing for use and import into gaming, VFX, and VR workflows.

- The model is now generally available after its initial September release in preview, offering both freemium and paid tiers starting at $20/mo.
